About Surface Finish Intelligence

Bridging AI and advanced machining through deep learning. Predict, analyze, and optimize surface roughness with precision.

Project Background

Our journey commenced with a profound realization: conventional methodologies for predicting surface finish frequently lack the desired level of accuracy. This inherent limitation often leads to a cascade of detrimental effects within manufacturing environments, including inflated production expenses, protracted setup durations, and a compromise in the overall quality of the final product. However, the emergence of artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML) has illuminated a transformative pathway for the precision machining sector. The potential to revolutionize this industry by leveraging these advanced technologies has become increasingly apparent.

With a central emphasis on achieving real-time prediction capabilities and dynamic optimization strategies, our proposed system is meticulously designed to empower manufacturers with the confidence necessary to consistently meet the increasingly complex and ever-changing demands of the contemporary market. By providing timely and accurate insights into surface finish outcomes, our solution will enable proactive adjustments to machining parameters, ultimately leading to enhanced efficiency, reduced waste, and superior product quality.

How It Works

The Surface Finish Intelligence platform operates on a deep learning-based framework that analyzes historical CNC machining data. We begin by gathering machining parameters like spindle speed, feed rate, depth of cut (DOC), and cutting force, which significantly impact the final surface quality. This data is processed through a trained TensorFlow model, which identifies patterns and relationships between these factors and the resulting surface roughness (Ra).

Once the model is trained, it can predict the surface roughness of future machining operations in real-time, with minimal human intervention. The platform not only provides predictions but also offers optimization suggestions to improve machining performance and reduce defects. As more data is collected, the model continues to learn and refine its predictions, offering even more accurate and reliable results over time.
